Cynthia Khanyisile Msibi is a third-year Biomedical Engineering student at <a href="https://www.kocaeli.edu.tr/">Kocaeli University</a>’s Faculty of Technology in Turkey. Her interests include biomedical engineering, rehabilitation technologies, mechanical design, and soft robotics.

She is a member of the mechanical unit of the <a href="https://www.instagram.com/kouhyperush/">KOU HypeRush Car Levitation Team</a>, where she focuses primarily on the design and production of the levitation rail system. In parallel, she is developing a wearable arm rehabilitation system, further strengthening her interest in assistive and rehabilitation technologies.

As part of her compulsory summer internship, Cynthia joined the <a href="https://riteh.uniri.hr/">University of Rijeka, Faculty of Engineering</a>, Croatia, as a visiting student intern. Under the supervision of Dr. Ervin Kamenar, she will work on soft robotics and rehabilitation-oriented EMG applications within the projects <a href="https://ekamenar.github.io/projects/HRZZ_2024_ASoRo/">Advanced soft robots: data-driven development, modeling and control</a> and <a href="https://ekamenar.github.io/projects/UNIRI_2025_EMG/">Koopman-driven real-time sEMG signal decomposition for robotic rehabilitation</a>.
